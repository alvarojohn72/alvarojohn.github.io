How the creators thought of 'GHIDORAH' 

Toho’s creators originally conceptualized King Ghidorah as an ancient, apocalyptic space dragon sent to destroy civilizations. Inspired by the mythological eight-headed beast Yamata no Orochi from Japanese folklore and the multi-headed Hydra, they designed him with three heads, bat-like wings, and a golden scale color to emphasize his "space-god" status. In his 1964 debut, creators envisioned him as an ancient, planet-eating cosmic entity responsible for wiping out life on Venus. He was treated as an extinction-level event that forced classic rivals Godzilla, Rodan, and Mothra to team up. In Godzilla vs. King Ghidorah, creators changed his origins to a time-traveling paradox. He began as three cute, genetically engineered pets from the future called "Dorats," which were fused and mutated by atomic radiation into the three-headed monster. In Godzilla, Mothra and King Ghidorah: Giant Monsters All-Out Attack, directors flipped the script completely. To make a film where Godzilla was the villain, the creators made Ghidorah a benevolent, heroic dragon and an ancient protector of Japan. For Godzilla: King of the Monsters, director Michael Dougherty and his design team envisioned Ghidorah as an "alpha" invasive species. To make the creature feel unique against a modern backdrop of cinematic dragons, the creators had three different actors use motion-capture for his three distinct heads, giving him varied, snake-like personalities
